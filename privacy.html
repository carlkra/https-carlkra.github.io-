Privacy & Analytics

TL;DR: This website uses privacy-friendly analytics that respect your data. No invasive tracking, no cross-site surveillance, no personal data collection.

My Approach to Web Analytics

As a Digital Analytics Specialist, I believe in the importance of understanding how visitors interact with websites—but not at the expense of user privacy. This site serves as a demonstration of privacy-first analytics implementation.

What Analytics Does This Site Use?

Currently Testing:

  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4) - Traditional analytics platform, requires cookie consent
  • Plausible Analytics - Privacy-focused alternative, no cookies required

Note: This is a testing environment where I experiment with different analytics implementations. Your consent preferences control which platforms can track your visit.

Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

GA4 is used on this site with your explicit consent. If you accept analytics cookies, GA4 collects:

  • Pages you visit and time spent on each page
  • How you arrived at this site (referral source)
  • General location (city/region level only)
  • Device and browser information
Your Control: You can accept or decline GA4 tracking via the cookie consent banner. Your choice is respected and remembered for future visits.

Plausible Analytics

Plausible is a lightweight, open-source analytics tool that doesn't require cookie consent because:

  • No cookies - Uses anonymous tracking without storing data on your device
  • No personal data - Doesn't collect IP addresses, browser fingerprints, or any personally identifiable information
  • No cross-site tracking - Your activity on this site stays on this site
  • GDPR compliant by design - Meets European privacy standards without requiring consent banners

Plausible collects only aggregated metrics like page views, referral sources, and general geographic regions. All data is anonymous and cannot be used to identify individual visitors.

✓ No cookies • No personal data • GDPR compliant

Implementation Details

This website uses Google Tag Manager (GTM) to manage all analytics implementations. GTM itself doesn't collect analytics data—it's simply a tool that loads and configures other analytics platforms based on your consent choices.

How Consent Works

When you visit this site, you'll see a consent banner powered by Klaro!, an open-source consent management solution. Here's what happens:

  1. Plausible loads immediately (no consent needed)
  2. GA4 waits for your consent choice
  3. If you accept, GA4 begins tracking with Google Consent Mode enabled
  4. If you decline, GA4 never loads
  5. Your choice is stored locally and respected on future visits

Why This Matters

As someone who implements analytics solutions professionally, I believe it's important to demonstrate that effective web analytics doesn't require invasive tracking. This site proves you can gather meaningful insights while respecting user privacy.

What I Learn From Analytics

The analytics on this site help me understand:

  • Which projects or content resonate with visitors
  • How people navigate through the site
  • Whether the site is accessible and performant
  • General traffic trends and referral sources

None of this requires knowing who you are—only how the site is being used.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Change your consent preferences at any time (use the privacy icon in the bottom corner)
  • Browse this site without any tracking (decline all analytics)
  • Know exactly what data is collected (it's detailed above)
  • Request deletion of any data (though Plausible doesn't collect personal data to delete)

Questions?

If you have questions about how this site handles analytics or privacy, feel free to reach out. I'm always happy to discuss privacy-first analytics implementation.
